About Me

I am Luis Chachayma, a Computer Engineering by the University of Kansas. I previously earned a degree in Electronics Engineering at the Pontificia Universidad Católica del Perú in 2014. After graduation I worked in the area of Substation Automation and Controls at ABB Group in Lima, Perú. This last summer I had an internship in an Automotive company (Standard Motor Products). I chose this company in order to learn more about automation in the automotive industry.

During my 2 years at ABB Group and Nakama Soluciones S.A.C., I worked in the area of Power Systems. My work was focused on the design of SCADA systems for the operation and supervision of high voltage electrical substations in power generation, distribution and transmission. This involved the integration of a variety of automation equipment, such as: PLCs, protection relays, firewalls, switches, RTUs, etc.

During my internship at Standard Motor Products I developed two projects: The first project was about the automation of the Melexis-Pace pressure calibrator with LabVIEW. Previously, the process was done manually, which involved many steps generating errors on the calibration, consequently the manufacturing scrap was high. My second project was about the automation of a Hydraulic-pressure Closing Press. This involved PLC programming, safety devices, and vision acquisition systems. This project consisted of the addition of a vision system to an existing project, helping the operator to reduce the production time. Due to the development of these successful projects, I have received recognition from the Engineering Manager and the Plant Manager for my contribution to the team.

During my last year at the University of Kansas, I decided to join the Jayhawk Motorsports Club, participating as a volunteer. I started in the team of the Combustion Car. I have been assigned several tasks that are detailed on my Portfolio. Recently this year I joined the Electric Car Team working with the Motor Controller, which is still in development.

Consequently, because of my experiences during my second degree I found what I am passionate about: cars. I want to apply my knowledge and experience earned in power systems to the automotive industry. Therefore, I am looking for full time opportunities in the automotive industry.
