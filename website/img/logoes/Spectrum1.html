About Me

I am Luis Chachayma a recent graduate in Computer Engineering at the University of Kansas. I already got a degree in Electronics Engineering at the Pontificia Universidad Católica del Perú I have been working in the area of Substation Automation and Controls at ABB Group. This last summer I had an internship in an Automotive company (Standard Motor Products). I chose this company in order to learn more about automation in automotive industry.

After I completed my first degree, I worked for 2 years in the area of Power Systems for ABB Group and Nakama Soluciones S.A.C. My work was focused on the design of SCADA systems for the operation and supervision of high voltage electrical substation in generation, distribution and transmission. This involved the integration a variety automation equipment, such as: PLCs, protection relays, firewalls, switches, RTUs, etc.

During my second degree at the University of Kansas, I had an internship at Standard Motor Products. During the internship, I developed two projects: The first project was about the automation of the Melexis-Pace pressure calibrator with LabVIEW. Previously, the process was done manually and the manual process involved many steps generating faults on the calibration, consequently the manufacturing scrap was high. My second project was about the automation of a Hydraulic-pressure Closing Press. This involved PLC programming, safety devices, and vision acquisition systems. This project consisted on the addition of a vision system to an existing project helping the operator to reduce the production time. Due to the development of these successful projects, I have received recognition from the Engineering Manager and the Plant Manager for my contribution to the team.

During my last year at the University of Kansas, I decided to be a part of the Jayhawk Motorsports Club, participating as a volunteer. I started in the team of the Combustion Car. I have been assigned several tasks that are detailed on my Portfolio. Lately this 2018 I joined the Electric Car Team working with the Motor Controller, which is still on development.

Consequently, of my experiences during my second degree I found what I am passionate about: cars. I want to apply my knowledge and experience earned in power systems to the automotive industry. Therefore, I am looking for full time opportunities in the automotive industry.
